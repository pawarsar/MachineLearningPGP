Context
The movie rating system that film buffs know today has been around for over 50 years. Over the years, the cultural standards and norms have changed and so have movie ratings. However, even today the process of rating a film remains a closely guarded industry secret.


Objective
MovieLens is a company in the internet and entertainment domain that has an online database of information related to films, television series, online streaming content – including cast, production crew, trivia, ratings, and fan and critical reviews. You have been hired as a Data Scientist for the company. You have been provided with the following three datasets, asked to carry out a detailed analysis of the data, and come up with some meaningful insights which will help the company to address their users in a better way.

 

Key steps to be performed
Import all the necessary packages
Read all the datasets into pandas dataframes
Get a brief overview of the datasets
Explore the datasets to come up with useful insights

Business Outcomes of the Case Study
Improved User Engagement: By analyzing ratings and merging them with user demographics, MovieLens can recommend movies that align with personal tastes. This personalization keeps users more engaged on the platform.
Example: A 22-year-old user who frequently rates sci-fi movies highly will automatically see new sci-fi releases recommended first.

Data-Driven Content Strategy: With insights from genre trends and rating patterns, MovieLens can decide which types of movies to promote more prominently. This helps align offerings with audience demand.
Example: If ratings show that “Comedy” is consistently top-rated among working professionals, MovieLens can prioritize promoting comedy titles to that segment.

Scalable Analysis for Large Datasets: Using NumPy and Pandas, MovieLens can efficiently process millions of rows of ratings and reviews without performance issues. This ensures the system can grow with user demand.
Example: Even with millions of new ratings each week, Pandas groupby functions can still compute average ratings per genre within seconds.

Enhanced Decision-Making for Partnerships: Analyzing user preferences allows MovieLens to form stronger partnerships with studios and streaming platforms, negotiating based on data-backed insights.
Example: If thrillers show high engagement in the 18–25 demographic, MovieLens can collaborate with a streaming service to create thriller content.

Increased Customer Satisfaction: With structured data and targeted recommendations, users feel understood and valued, leading to higher satisfaction and loyalty.
Example: A parent who often rates children’s movies highly will consistently see family-friendly suggestions, improving their trust in MovieLens recommendations.


Use Cases Across Other Industries
Entertainment & Media: Companies like Netflix, Hulu, and Disney+ use Pandas DataFrames to store, merge, and analyze user ratings and genre preferences for content recommendations.
Example: Netflix can combine user ratings (rating.csv) with movie details (movie.csv) to suggest personalized movie lists.

E-commerce & Retail: Platforms like Amazon analyze product reviews and ratings using NumPy arrays and Pandas operations to improve product recommendations.
Example: A list of product ratings [5, 4, 3, 5, 4] can be looped through to calculate the average rating of a product, helping the platform highlight top-rated products.

Social Media: Platforms like YouTube or TikTok organize engagement data (likes, comments, shares) into Pandas Series for quick trend analysis.
Example: YouTube can combine user demographic info (user.csv) with video ratings to understand which age group prefers certain content.

Healthcare: Hospitals track patient recovery times after surgery using NumPy arrays and Pandas DataFrames to analyze treatment effectiveness.
Example: A NumPy array of recovery days [7, 10, 5, 12, 8] can be passed into a function to calculate the average recovery time. Using Pandas, a DataFrame combining patient_id, treatment_type, and recovery_days can be created, then grouped by treatment type to find which procedure results in the fastest recovery.

Travel & Hospitality: Companies like Airbnb and TripAdvisor analyze customer reviews and ratings using NumPy statistical functions (mean, median) for service improvement.
Example: Airbnb can calculate the average rating of hosts across locations to highlight “Superhosts” for potential travelers.
